# Standard NDA — Review Notes and Flagged Clauses

## Document: Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (Standard Form)

### Clause 3 — Definition of Confidential Information

**Status**: ACCEPTABLE with standard carve-outs

The definition is appropriately broad but includes the standard exclusions (publicly known, independently developed, received from third parties without restriction). No issues.

### Clause 7 — Intellectual Property and Work Product

**Status**: FLAGGED — REQUIRES REVISION

The current draft states: *"Any work product, analysis, or intellectual property developed in connection with or using Confidential Information shall be the sole property of the Disclosing Party."*

**Issue**: This language is overly broad and would assign ownership of our internal analysis, methodologies, and deliverables to the client. This creates significant IP exposure — especially for proprietary valuation models and advisory frameworks.

**Recommended revision**: Narrow to: "Work product that incorporates or is derived directly from Confidential Information shall remain the property of the Disclosing Party to the extent of the Confidential Information contained therein. Pre-existing methodologies, tools, and intellectual property of the Receiving Party are expressly excluded."

### Clause 9 — Term and Survival

**Status**: ACCEPTABLE

2-year term with 5-year survival on core IP obligations. This is industry standard.

### Clause 11 — Remedies

**Status**: FLAG FOR NEGOTIATION

Injunctive relief language is standard but note the lack of a cure period for inadvertent disclosure. We should request a 5-business-day notice and cure provision before injunctive relief can be sought.

### Clause 14 — Governing Law

**Status**: ACCEPTABLE (Delaware — our standard preference)

## Summary Recommendation

Do not execute without revising Clause 7. All other clauses are acceptable as drafted. Estimate 1-2 rounds of negotiation to resolve the IP issue — Henderson's counsel has been reasonable to work with on prior NDAs.
