# Subcontractor Agreement — Legal Risk Assessment

## Overview

This document summarizes key legal risks identified in our standard subcontractor agreements for construction advisory engagements where we act as project manager or owner's representative.

## Identified Risks

### 1. Indemnification Scope — HIGH RISK

**Issue**: Current clause requires us to indemnify the subcontractor for "any and all claims arising from the project" including claims caused by the subcontractor's own negligence.

**Risk Level**: High — we should only indemnify for claims arising from our own negligence.

**Recommended Action**: Revise to mutual proportional indemnification: each party indemnifies for its own acts and omissions only.

### 2. Consequential Damages Cap — MEDIUM RISK

**Issue**: The consequential damages waiver is one-sided — we waive consequential damages against the subcontractor but the subcontractor retains the right to claim lost profits against us.

**Risk Level**: Medium

**Recommended Action**: Make the waiver mutual. In no event should either party be liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages.

### 3. Insurance Requirements — MEDIUM RISK

**Issue**: Minimum GL coverage of $1M per occurrence is below industry standard for projects over $10M. Projects in the $100M+ range typically require $5M minimum.

**Recommended Action**: Scale insurance requirements to contract value: $1M (<$10M), $2M ($10-50M), $5M (>$50M).

### 4. Dispute Resolution — LOW RISK

**Issue**: Current clause requires litigation in the subcontractor's home state, which creates geographic inconvenience.

**Recommended Action**: Require arbitration (AAA rules) in a neutral jurisdiction (typically Delaware or New York for national engagements).

### 5. IP and Deliverables Ownership

**Issue**: No explicit clause addressing ownership of project deliverables, drawings, and data.

**Risk Level**: Medium (see NDA review notes for related IP concerns)

**Recommended Action**: Add explicit clause: all project deliverables, reports, and analysis produced under this agreement are works made for hire and are owned by the client upon full payment.

## Summary

The agreement requires moderate revision before it's suitable for projects above $25M in value. The indemnification clause is the priority fix. Estimated legal cost to revise: $2,500-$4,000 (2-3 attorney hours).
