# Commercial Real Estate Market — Q1 2026 Analysis

## Market Overview

Commercial real estate fundamentals diverged sharply by sector in Q1 2026. Industrial and multi-family assets continued to outperform while traditional office remained under structural pressure. Retail showed unexpected resilience in experiential and grocery-anchored formats.

## Office Sector

National office vacancy reached 19.4%, the highest since 2010. Class A trophy assets in CBD locations maintained occupancy above 90% while suburban Class B saw vacancy rates exceeding 30%. Conversion to residential use accelerated — 47 million sq ft of office space is in active conversion pipeline. Effective rents declined 8% YoY on a net basis after free rent concessions and TI allowances.

## Industrial Sector

Industrial vacancy tightened to 4.1% nationally. Last-mile distribution in major metros is essentially full. New supply of 340 million sq ft under construction will provide relief in H2 2026. Average asking rents increased 11% YoY. Cap rates compressed 25-50 bps for institutional-grade product.

## Retail Sector

Grocery-anchored strip centers achieved 97% occupancy — highest in 15 years. Experiential retail (entertainment, fitness, medical) absorbed most available big-box space. Power center vacancies declined as off-price and discount tenants expanded. E-commerce penetration stabilized at 23%, removing the primary demand headwind.

## Multi-Family

Apartment demand remained robust driven by household formation and homeownership affordability constraints. New supply of 600,000 units came online in 2025 causing rent growth to moderate to 2.1%. Cap rates range 4.25-5.5% for stabilized assets. Build-to-rent single-family continued to attract institutional capital.

## Capital Markets

Transaction volume declined 18% YoY as bid-ask spreads remain wide. CMBS spreads tightened 40 bps in Q1. Debt availability improved for high-quality industrial and multi-family. Construction lending remained tight — only the strongest sponsors with pre-leasing achieved favorable terms.

## Key Takeaway

Capital is rotating from office/retail to industrial and multi-family. Investors willing to underwrite conversion risk in office-to-residential are finding attractive basis. Distressed opportunities are emerging in suburban office markets.
