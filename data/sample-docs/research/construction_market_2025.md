# Construction Industry Market Report — 2025

## Executive Summary

The construction industry recorded $2.1 trillion in total spending in 2025, a 4.7% increase over 2024. Growth was concentrated in infrastructure, data centers, and industrial facilities, while residential construction softened in the second half due to elevated mortgage rates.

## Key Trends

### Infrastructure Spending
Federal infrastructure allocations drove $380 billion in highway, bridge, and transit projects. States with accelerated permitting saw 30-40% faster project starts. Public-private partnerships increased to 22% of total infrastructure value.

### Data Center Construction
Data center construction grew 68% year-over-year, driven by AI infrastructure demand. Average data center project size increased from $120M to $310M. Power infrastructure co-location became a standard requirement — projects near existing substations commanded 15-20% premiums.

### Industrial and Logistics
Warehouse and distribution center construction remained at record levels. Near-shoring and reshoring drove $90B in new manufacturing facility construction. Cold chain and food-grade facilities saw the highest margins in the segment.

### Labor and Materials
Labor shortages persisted across skilled trades — electricians, plumbers, and ironworkers saw 12-18% wage increases. Material costs stabilized after the 2022-2023 inflation spike. Steel and lumber returned to pre-pandemic baselines; copper remained elevated due to EV and grid demand.

## Regional Highlights

- **Sunbelt**: Texas, Florida, and Arizona led in project volume; all three saw double-digit growth
- **Midwest**: Manufacturing reshoring projects concentrated in Ohio, Michigan, and Indiana
- **Northeast**: Urban infill and adaptive reuse dominated; ground-up commercial softened

## Outlook for 2026

Forward indicators suggest moderate growth of 3-5%. Infrastructure backlog remains at 14-month highs. Risks include interest rate sensitivity for private commercial projects and continued labor availability constraints.
